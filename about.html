About Me

In 2005, I joined the United States Army as a Heavy Equipment Operator (21E). I was stationed at Fort Bragg, North Carolina and assigned to the 37th Engineering Battalion. In 2006, I deployed to Afghanistan in support of Operation Enduring Freedom. After returning, I met and married my wife Victoria in April of 2009. In June of 2009, I deployed to Iraq in support of Operation Iraqi Freedom. Upon return, my original enlistment contract had expired and I decided to look for a more technical career path in the Air Force Reserve.


In the Air Force Reserve I served as an avionics technician for communication and navigation systems on heavy aircraft from 2010 to 2019. During that time I became an Air Reserve Technician (ART), working full-time for my assigned unit and gained experience on KC-10, C-5, and KC-135 airframes. Along the way I cross-trained and became qualified in guidence and control systems and advanced to the rank of E-6.


In December of 2016, I remained a traditional reservist with my unit but took full-time employment with the Federal Aviation Addministration (FAA). As a technician with the FAA, I performed depot level repair on National Air Space (NAS) equipment. This included Air Surveillance Radar (ASR), Air Route Surveillance Radar (ARSR), communication, weather, and airfield lighting systems.


Our family's chance to follow our dream of living abroad came in 2019. I took a position with a business consulting firm that allowed me to work 100% remotely. We chose Morocco after having visited for vacation. My wife and I had always wanted to return and raise our family in a rich and diverse culture. It's climate, terrain, people, and close proximity to Europe make it a beautiful home.
